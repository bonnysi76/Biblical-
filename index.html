“The Furnace of Flesh”

A Divine Comedy-style Lament on Sin, Lust, and Agony

“In sin did my mother conceive me” — so I cried,
From the mire of my soul, where the light had long died (Psalm 51:5).
I was drawn like a lamb, not to slaughter—but shame,
Led captive by lust, and my bones burned with flame (James 1:14-15).

My iniquities rise like a flood in my chest,
“Too heavy for me” — I find no rest (Psalm 38:4).
I am “a worm and not a man,” despised of the race (Psalm 22:6),
And demons have hungered to tear off my face.

I sought You in groanings that break through the night (Romans 8:26),
Yet my eyes failed with watching for Your light (Psalm 69:3).
“Create in me a clean heart,” I cry in despair (Psalm 51:10),
But my prayers fall back down, dissolved in the air (Lamentations 3:44).

I thirst for You “as in a dry land” (Psalm 63:1),
But my hands are defiled, and can’t touch the Lamb (Isaiah 59:2-3).
My flesh cries aloud like a beast in a cage,
“Deliver me from this body of death and rage!” (Romans 7:24).

“Woe to the rebellious children,” says the Lord (Isaiah 30:1),
And I—the prodigal—spend all I can hoard.

The harlot has beckoned with lips dipped in lies (Proverbs 5:3),
I followed her chamber down death’s disguise (Proverbs 7:27).

I wept with Esau, seeking place for repentance (Hebrews 12:17),
But found none, only silence and stiffened resistance.
My conscience is seared (1 Timothy 4:2), my affections defiled (Titus 1:15),
And lust whispers sweet in the voice of the vile.

The plague of my heart—no physician can cure (Jeremiah 17:9),
No balm in Gilead, no ointment pure (Jeremiah 8:22).
I fasted, I wept, I tore robes in vain,
But my sin, like scarlet, still leaves the same stain (Isaiah 1:18).

“They have eyes full of adultery,” You said (2 Peter 2:14),
“They never cease sinning,” and their spirits are dead.

I am them. I am he. I am dust and despair.
“Why have You forsaken me?” hangs in the air (Matthew 27:46).

My soul cleaves to the dust—revive me by Word (Psalm 119:25),
But my ears are too dull; Your voice is not heard (Isaiah 6:10).
I am “given to the desires of my heart” (Romans 1:24),
And my hands build again what once was torn apart (Galatians 2:18).

“Wash me,” I beg, “and I shall be clean” (Psalm 51:7),
But the waters run red—still cursed and unclean (Leviticus 13:45).
Your Spirit withdrew, and Ichabod stayed,
And demons sat feasting where my conscience decayed (1 Samuel 4:21).

I remembered Your law but delighted no more (Psalm 1:2),
My fruit rotted slowly from the root to the core (Matthew 7:19).
Though I “buffet my body and bring it to slave” (1 Corinthians 9:27),
It rises again, like a corpse from the grave.

“Why do You stand far off, O Lord?” (Psalm 10:1)
“Why hide in times of trouble?”—Have I not implored?
With sackcloth of mourning I covered my face (Psalm 35:13),
But sin laughs in echoes and mocks all my grace.

Yet still I remember the days of Your hand (Psalm 143:5),
When You “brought me up from the miry land” (Psalm 40:2).
Will You forget me forever, O King? (Psalm 13:1)
Shall I dwell in the pit where no angels sing?

Though I fall seven times, can I rise again? (Proverbs 24:16)
Or am I the dog returning to sin? (2 Peter 2:22).
My tears have been food both day and by night (Psalm 42:3),
And still I grope blindly, shut off from Your light.

I see no deliverer—none righteous remains (Romans 3:10),
Only “wretched man that I am,” bound in chains (Romans 7:24).
Is there balm for the broken? Hope for the lost?
Or was I ordained to be damned from the Cross?

But even now… I remember Your Name (Psalm 119:55),
And though I am ash, still I call just the same.
If grace is for sinners, then who sins like me? (Luke 5:32)
I stretch out my hands—O God, rescue me! (Psalm 143:6)
🕯️ End of Lament
